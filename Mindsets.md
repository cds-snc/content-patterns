# Mindsets

## What are mindsets

Mindsets are a way of understanding people’s attitudes, behaviours and motivations in certain situations. As situations change so do our mindsets. 

## Why are we using mindsets? 

Understanding the factors that determine specific mindsets allows us to create products and services that are tailored to different people’s attitudes in different contexts of use. 

## Notify mindset

### The Matrix
Mindsets are constructed in a two-by-two matrix. Each axis represents a range of a singular attitude that will affect how individuals view and use products and services. 

X axis: Independent - Collaborative

Independent means:
Independent decision-maker
Self-sufficient for executing work
Self-reliant when stuck
Acts mostly without consulting others
Has access only to own tools and assets

Collaborative means:
Makes decisions collaboratively
Gets things done through team efforts
When stuck, can turn to team for support
Depends on coordination with team when acting
Has access to shared tools and assets


Y axis: Takes freedom to innovate - Follows standard procedures

Takes freedom to innovate means:
Works within a flexible framework
Exploration driven (curious)
Adjusts processes to people
Customizes features & templates
Uses multiple tools

Follows standard procedures means:
Works within a fixed framework
Purpose driven (results)
Strives to fit into existing processes
Uses defaults & templates
Uses one main tool

### The quadrants

The quadrants are representative of the mindsets themselves, differentiating the attitudes and behaviours of users as they move through their day, month, year.

1. Explorative

When in an explorative mindset, users are willing to embrace the unknown in their search for better solutions and processes. They are dealing with multiple variables and are not afraid to learn from mistakes along the way. 

I value frameworks that allow experimentation
I value learning through trial & error
I value the freedom to adjust my process on the fly

I need to know the what, how and why before taking action
I need reassurance, confirmation and follow-up along the way
I need you to flex and adjust as I learn and improve


2. Adaptable

When in a adaptable mindset, users are constantly reviewing and improving their processes. They feel empowered to make decisions, learn through feedback and don’t hesitate to ask for support when feeling stuck. 

I value having a team to reassure and support me
I value interchangeable roles within an agile team
I embrace ‘a good challenge’

I need customizable components that fit in multiple systems
I need an open and collaborative environment in which I can grow
I need adaptable tools and processes that accommodate multiple goals and working styles

3.Conventional

When in a conventional mindset, users are seeking the guarantee of success in the procedures and service standards that they follow. They trust that if they do their part diligently, the task will get completed as planned. 

I value clear implementation standards that have been tested to work
I value in-task guidance, direct and focused only on what I’m doing
I value well defined roles and responsibilities within the team

I need to be guided by clear standards and instructions
I need to understand what my role is and what my responsibilities are within the team
I need to tools that I know how to control in order to keep things moving

4. Conservative

When in a conservative mindset, users are determined to complete the task at hand. They favor taking the habitual path to results, despite it not always being the shortest way there. 

I value  continuity and known frameworks
I value reliable solutions and processes that I know to have worked before
I value certainty over efficiency

I need to feel in control so I know it will work
I don’t need distractions from my priorities
I need certainty and stability when it comes to the tools that I use
