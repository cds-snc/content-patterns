# Attach files: Content Variations

## Variation 1: Direct and Standard (Conventional Mindset)
*Focus: Clear instructions, task-oriented guidance, and role clarity.*

### Title: Attach files to your email
Upload files to send with your email template. Recipients get the files directly in their inbox.

**Before you send**
- Recipients keep their own copy of these files indefinitely.
- You cannot revoke or update a file once it has been sent.

**File requirements**
- **Types:** .pdf, .doc, .docx, .odt, .rtf, .txt, .xlsx, .csv, .json, .jpeg, .jpg, .png
- **Limit:** Up to 10 files
- **Size:** Combined total must not exceed 6 MB

## Variation 2: Contextual and Reassuring (Explorative/Adaptable Mindsets)
*Focus: Explain the "why", provide context, and help users understand the implications of their actions.*

### Title: Adding files to your template
Sharing files with your recipients is easy, but keep in mind that once sent, these files belong to the recipient's inbox.

**How it works**
When you attach a file, it is delivered alongside your email. Because recipients receive an actual copy, you won't be able to "unsend" or edit the file after it reaches them.

**What you can upload**
We support common document types (.pdf, .doc, .docx, .odt, .rtf, .txt), spreadsheets (.xlsx, .csv, .json), and images (.jpeg, .jpg, .png).

- **Quantity:** You can add up to 10 files.
- **Total size:** Keep the total under 6 MB to ensure delivery.

## Design Principle Alignment: Guidance
This draft meets the **Guidance** principle by:
1. **Reducing risk of error:** By explicitly stating that files cannot be revoked or updated, we prevent users from making a choice (sending a draft or sensitive file) that they can't undo.
2. **Providing context of use:** Explaining that recipients keep the copy indefinitely helps the sender understand the long-term impact of the "Send" action.
3. **In-task guidance:** The clear breakdown of file types and limits provides the "what and how" exactly when the user needs it to move forward.
