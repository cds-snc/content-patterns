# Notify design principles

1. Build trust with recipients. 
2. Be reliable to senders.



## Clarity

Offer me straightforward information and actionable instructions, in simple and clear language. 

For recipients:
Use words, phrases and concepts familiar to me
Be consistent in the way you address me
Speak to me in the language of my choice
Send me relevant, tailored information
Be consistently clear about the process steps

For senders:
Provide clear and actionable instructions
Help me understand when to send notifications
Offer me useful templates tailored to my needs
Provide support that is focused on my task
Show me how with easy to understand examples

Recipients are experiencing difficulty in navigating government services and often feel misunderstood or lacking control.

Senders need simple means and clear guidance to easily reach the public. 

Government needs to better connect with the public. 

## Efficiency

Help me be fast and efficient so that I can save time and avoid unnecessary delays. 

For recipients
Enable me to anticipate upcoming steps
Make sure your messages reach me
Only ask of me what is necessary
Tailor information to my situation
Provide me with flexible options

For senders
Enable me to do simple tasks without laborious work
Offer the right channels to connect with people
Couple new tools with legacy systems
Create tools that are flexible, extensible and error-free
Provide short and efficient documentation

Recipients would reduce the need to call and wait on hold

Senders could reduce delays in an already lengthy process. 

Government could reduce the costs while improving the impact of its services.


## Guidance

Guide me through this process so that I can do the right thing at the right time. 

For recipients
Provide helpful wayfinding at every phase
Help me make the right choice from the very start
Reassure me I am on the right path
Point me towards the next steps
Connect me to other services I need

For senders
Provide concrete guidance and tailored support
Don’t offer choices that won’t work
Reduce my risks of making an error
When errors occur, constructively suggest a solution
Provide context of use for a tool or a task

Recipients are confused about the choices available to them and the steps of the process itself. 

Senders spend additional time on the phone guiding receivers through a confusing process.

Government could reduce frustration on the part of both senders and receivers. 

## Transparency

Be transparent so that I can understand the process and have realistic expectations.

For recipients
Let me see the process so that I know what to expect
Give me context when providing an update
Provide me with a way to track progress
Be open about the process/follow-up
Explain what you need from me and why

Show me all the steps to complete a task
Give me immediate feedback
Give me information from sources I trust
Be clear about the purpose of a process or task
Help me facilitate the dialogue with the public

Recipients are too often forced to live with a lot of unknowns (and stress).

Senders can decrease uncertainty and stress with additional information not currently communicated.

Government needs to increase satisfaction levels with the government. 

## Trust

Help me build a reliable connection with you through consistent actions and predictable steps. 

For recipients
Follow-up with updates regularly
Be the source of truth when I have a question
Hear me out and give me personalized advice
Make sure I got and understood your message
Send relevant and appropriate updates

For senders
Report on status of activity
Enable me to (initiate) follow-up
Maintain consistency among processes/channels
Empower me to be a trustworthy source of information
Enable me to personalize the messages I send

Recipients are using unofficial sources of information and often end up misinformed. 

Senders need reliable tools to facilitate the dialogue between the government and the public. 

GoC needs to be a reliable, trustworthy source of information.
